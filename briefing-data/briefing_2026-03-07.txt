Week 4: Bispecific Antibodies & Protein Engineering
Day 1 (MECHANISM): Format Diversity -- The 100+ Architectures That
Define How Bispecifics Work

At their core, bispecific antibodies (bsAbs) can bind two distinct
antigens or epitopes simultaneously -- a capability impossible for
conventional monospecific antibodies. This single engineering insight
has driven the creation of over 100 distinct molecular formats, each
with specific manufacturing, pharmacokinetic, and clinical tradeoffs.
Understanding format diversity is essential for evaluating any bispecific
deal or investment.

THE THREE MAJOR FORMAT FAMILIES

1. IgG-Like Formats (with Fc region)
   These retain the antibody's constant region (Fc), conferring long
   half-life (typically 2-3 weeks) via FcRn recycling and enabling
   effector functions (ADCC, CDC). The engineering challenge: in a
   standard IgG with two distinct heavy chains and two distinct light
   chains, the cell can produce 10 possible pairings -- only 1 is
   correct.
   Solutions:
   - Knobs-into-holes (Genentech/Roche): Engineered CH3 steric bumps
     force correct heavy-chain heterodimerization. Most widely licensed
     format; used in mosunetuzumab (CD20xCD3), faricimab (ANG2xVEGF).
   - CrossMab (Roche): Swaps CH1/CL or VH/VL domains on one arm to
     solve light-chain mispairing. Used in cibisatamab.
   - Common light chain approach: Both arms share the same light chain,
     eliminating mispairing. Used by Merus (Zenocutuzumab, MCLA-128).
   - SEED body: Strand-exchange engineered domains for asymmetric Fc.
   Examples: amivantamab (EGFR x MET, Janssen/J&J), faricimab
   (ANG2 x VEGF, Roche), mosunetuzumab (CD20 x CD3, Roche),
   ivonescimab (PD-1 x VEGF, Akeso/Summit).

2. Fragment-Based Formats (no Fc)
   Remove the Fc region entirely, creating smaller molecules with
   faster tissue penetration but short serum half-life (hours).
   - BiTE (Bispecific T-cell Engager, Amgen): Tandem scFvs connected
     by a flexible linker. MW ~55kDa. Classic example: blinatumomab
     (CD19 x CD3, ALL). Requires continuous IV infusion (9mL/hr
     pump) due to 2-hour half-life. Half-life extended BiTEs (HLE-BiTEs)
     now in development.
   - DART (Dual-Affinity Re-Targeting, MacroGenics): Two scFvs with a
     disulfide bridge for improved stability vs. BiTE. Flotetuzumab
     (CD123 x CD3) for AML.
   - Diabodies: Two scFvs non-covalently associated in trans.
   Manufacturing advantage: E. coli expression possible for some formats;
   lower COGS but complex purification.

3. Multi-Specific and Asymmetric Formats (1+1, 2+1, trispecifics)
   - 1+1 bispecific: One binding site per antigen (the most common
     bsAb configuration). Standard format for T-cell engagers.
   - 2+1 format: Two binding domains for tumor antigen + one for CD3.
     The bivalency creates avidity for tumor targeting while maintaining
     monovalent CD3 engagement (reducing cytokine storm risk).
     Teclistamab (BCMA x CD3, J&J) and elranatamab (Pfizer) use this
     logic.
   - Trispecifics: Bind three targets simultaneously. Vertex-WuXi
     Biologics trispecific T-cell engager deal (Feb 2026) illustrates
     the bleeding edge of this field.

MANUFACTURING IMPLICATIONS FOR INVESTORS
The format choice directly determines manufacturing cost, partner
requirements, and competitive moat:
- Fragment-based (BiTE, DART): Lower COGS, E. coli possible, but
  PK limitations require dosing regimens that hurt patient convenience.
- IgG-like bispecifics: CHO expression preferred; yield and purity
  challenges are 2-5x harder than monospecifics; more CDMO expertise
  required. This creates manufacturing IP moats.
- Aggregation propensity: Novel format junctions create aggregation
  hotspots. More complex downstream processing. Immunogenicity risk
  from non-natural linker regions is real but manageable.

CLINICAL TRANSLATION PATTERNS
- T-cell engagers (CD3-redirecting) have proven the bispecific concept
  in heme malignancies; solid tumors remain the hard frontier (antigen
  heterogeneity, immunosuppressive TME).
- Dual checkpoint bispecifics (PD-1 x VEGF = ivonescimab; CTLA-4 x PD-1)
  are generating landmark data (ivonescimab beat Keytruda in PD-L1+ NSCLC
  in China).
- Non-oncology bispecifics: faricimab (ophthalmology), emicizumab
  (hemophilia A, FIXa x FX), showing the format's versatility.

INVESTMENT PATTERN TO REMEMBER
Format selection is an early-stage decision that becomes a permanent
competitive constraint. Ask: (1) What is the half-life strategy? (2) What
is the light-chain pairing solution and who owns the relevant patents?
(3) What manufacturing platform does the CDMO use and what is the yield
target? The answers reveal both scientific credibility and IP risk.
